Dutch Apple Pancake Recipe

Grandma Connie's Dutch Apple Pancake

Ooohhhh the sweet, savory, deliciousness of apples, butter, cinnamon, and cast iron. A flavorful combination to transport you back to your poor dutch roots. Remember, it's not the sum of the ingredients that matter, but rather, the mathematical proof, showing that it's possible to be done in the first place. Just remember - the amount of butter suggested in the recipe is simply a lower-limit. Go wildcats!

Ingredients

  • 1 Medium Apple
  • 1/4 Cup Butter
  • 2 Tblsp Sugar
  • 1/2 Tsp Ground Cinnamon
  • 1/4 Tsp Nutmeg
  • 1 Cup Biscuit Mix
  • 2/3 Cup Milk
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 Tblsp Sugar

Directions

  1. Core apple and slice, leaving skins on. Melt butter in pan and blend sugar, cinnamon & nutmeg
  2. Add apples and saute until tender
  3. Combine biscuit mix, milk, and egg. Pour over apples, turn heat down, cook 10 to 12 min - or until surface looks dull
  4. Place under broiler, one to two minutes, until lightly browned
  5. Serve upside down

Yield: 4 Servings
